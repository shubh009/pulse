Cardiologist Habits for Longevity and Vitality

A

Dr. Aaron Patel, FACC

Board-Certified Cardiologist, Longevity Institute

July 08, 2026 • 7 min read

Preventative cardiology is the most effective tool we have to increase healthspan. By addressing cardiovascular risk factors early through strategic lifestyle changes, we can prevent arterial blockages and maintain vascular elasticity into advanced age.

Aerobic Efficiency & Zone 2 Training

Cardiologists recommend building an aerobic base through Zone 2 training. Exercising at a pace where you can maintain a conversation but feel challenged increases your stroke volume, allowing the heart to pump more blood with fewer beats, reducing resting blood pressure.

"A low resting heart rate is a strong statistical predictor of cardiorespiratory longevity, indicating a highly efficient myocardial pump."

Endothelial Function & Nitric Oxide

Nitric oxide is the molecule responsible for dilating blood vessels and maintaining clear pathways. Boosting natural production through leafy greens, beetroot, and micro-movements throughout the workday protects against arterial stiffening and plaque accumulation.

By prioritizing vascular elasticity, stress regulation, and consistent aerobic conditioning, we can construct an ironclad defense against modern heart issues.
